Assignment 2 – Prompt Exercise

Objective

This assignment examines how different AI models respond to the same research task. The goal is to design prompts, evaluate model outputs, refine instructions, and understand how prompt structure influences the quality, accuracy, and depth of responses.

Design Prompt

For the first test, I submitted the following prompt to multiple AI models:

Write a 1,500-word literature review that reads like an analytical discussion rather than a textbook summary. Start by explaining the purpose and scope of the review. Include a methodology section describing how ideas, themes, and findings are compared across studies. Add key insights by identifying patterns, similarities, and differences. Highlight important trends, challenges, and meaningful research gaps. Finish by suggesting one realistic and testable hypothesis that logically follows from the discussion. Maintain a formal but clear writing style, prioritizing explanation and reasoning over definitions or lists.

The prompt was submitted to ChatGPT, Copilot, and Grok 3.

Model Response Analysis

Each model response was evaluated using the following criteria:

  • Structure
  • Synthesis
  • Trends and Gaps
  • Hypothesis Quality
  • References

Structure

ChatGPT demonstrated the strongest structure by clearly including a methodology section and organizing the response in a professional, systematic format. Copilot also performed well in this area, using clean headings that made the review easy to follow. In contrast, Grok 3 included a methodology paragraph but presented the response more like a continuous essay, lacking clearly defined sections expected in a structured review.

Synthesis

ChatGPT provided the most accurate synthesis of data mining and machine learning applications. It remained focused on the topic and discussed how researchers balance model accuracy with interpretability. Copilot failed in this area by shifting focus to technology in education rather than data mining. Similarly, Grok 3 emphasized AI’s impact on jobs and the economy, offering interesting insights but insufficient technical discussion of machine learning applications.

Trends and Gaps

All models attempted to identify trends, but ChatGPT was the most aligned with the prompt. It highlighted a shift from simple predictive models to more causal and explainable approaches. Copilot identified meaningful gaps related to education, but these were irrelevant to the assignment topic. Grok 3 introduced a strong contemporary trend, agentic AI, and correctly noted the lack of long-term evaluation of such systems.

Hypothesis Quality

The quality of hypotheses varied. ChatGPT proposed a clear and testable hypothesis regarding adaptive versus static models over time. Copilot also produced a well-structured hypothesis, but it was unrelated to data mining. Grok 3 failed to provide a hypothesis due to an incomplete response, which significantly weakened its overall performance.

References

All models struggled with references. None provided verifiable citations with authors, dates, or sources. Instead, they referred broadly to existing literature, which limits academic reliability and makes it difficult to verify claims using sources such as Google Scholar or Semantic Scholar.

Strengths and Weaknesses

Overall, ChatGPT’s main strength was its ability to follow instructions accurately while staying on topic, though it lacked verifiable citations. Copilot’s strength was its clear and organized structure, but it struggled significantly with topic relevance. Grok 3 stood out for offering unique and forward-looking insights, but its incomplete response and lack of structure limited its effectiveness.

Refined Prompts

ChatGPT

Write a 1,500-word literature review on data mining and machine learning. You must include real academic in-text citations (Author, Year) to support your arguments. Include a methodology section explaining how studies were selected and compared. Ensure synthesis across studies rather than simple description. Conclude with a clear, testable hypothesis and provide a full reference list at the end.

Copilot

Write an analytical literature review of exactly 1,500 words on the technical applications of data mining and machine learning. Focus strictly on computer science topics such as algorithms, model performance, and data patterns. Do not include discussions of human learning or education. Organize the review using clear sections: methodology, key findings, trends, and research gaps. End with a formal, testable hypothesis.

Grok 3

Imagine you are a data scientist writing a 2,000-word systematic literature review on data mining and machine learning in domains such as healthcare and finance. Include a clear methodology and focus on technical aspects like model accuracy and handling complex data. Ensure the response is complete, well-structured, and ends with a bold, testable hypothesis and a conclusion.

Final Prompt

I am providing three drafts of a literature review on data mining and machine learning. Combine them into one cohesive 2,000-word systematic review. Use the methodology and citations from the first draft, the structure and organization from the second, and the modern technical insights from the third. Ensure the final paper is well-structured, academically rigorous, and flows as a single unified document. Conclude with the most realistic and testable hypothesis.

Reflection

How did the models respond differently?

Each model approached the task differently. ChatGPT emphasized structure and technical accuracy, connecting historical and modern developments in data mining. Copilot focused on formatting and readability but initially struggled with topic alignment. Grok 3 provided more contemporary and forward-looking insights, particularly around emerging AI trends, but had difficulty maintaining completeness and structure in longer responses.

Which refinements worked best?

The most effective refinements were those that added specificity. Requiring ChatGPT to include real citations improved credibility significantly. For Copilot, explicitly restricting the topic ensured it stayed within the correct domain. For Grok 3, instructions about length and completeness helped prevent incomplete outputs and ensured inclusion of the hypothesis.

What did you learn?

This exercise demonstrated that AI-generated academic work requires active human guidance. Initial outputs often lack precision, accuracy, or completeness. By refining prompts, the quality improves significantly. It also showed that different models have different strengths, making it useful to combine outputs rather than rely on a single source. Ultimately, human oversight remains essential to verify accuracy, ensure relevance, and produce a reliable final review.
